WBJEE results 2025 expected to release soon.Check steps to download RANKCARD here:

WBJEE result 2025: The West Bengal Joint Entrance Examinations Board (WBJEEB) is expected to release the WBJEE Result 2025 shortly, bringing anxious anticipation to thousands of engineering aspirants across the state. The result will play a decisive role in admissions to undergraduate courses in engineering, architecture, and pharmacy in both government and private institutions across West Bengal.
Once declared, candidates will be able to access their WBJEE 2025 rank cards exclusively through the board’s official website — wbjeeb.nic.in. The board has reiterated that no results will be dispatched via post or made available on any third-party platform

The West Bengal Joint Entrance Examination Board (WBJEEB) has delayed the release of the WBJEE 2025 results, causing growing anxiety among one lakh candidates.
As per past year trends, the results are typically declared within 20 to 48 days after the exam. This year, the exam was held on April 28, and based on previous timelines, the result was expected in late May or early June. However, the delay has continued into July.
According to a report by Times Now, the results are currently on hold due to the Calcutta High Court’s interim stay on the revised OBC reservation list, which will remain in effect until July 31.

WBJEE result 2025: Steps to download rankcard-
Candidates can follow the procedure outlined below to download their scorecards:

  • Visit the official WBJEEB website: wbjeeb.nic.in.
  • On the homepage, locate and click the link labeled “WBJEE 2025 Result” or “Download Rank Card.” or on the home page,click on the "EXAMINATIONS" ribon then click on "WBJEE"then locate and click on “WBJEE 2025 Result” or “Download Rank Card.”
  • Enter your application number, password or date of birth, and the captcha code as prompted.
  • Click on the ‘Submit’ or ‘Login’ button to proceed.
  • Your WBJEE 2025 rank card will appear on the screen. Download it and keep multiple printouts for counselling and admission purposes.
